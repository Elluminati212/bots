**Russia-Ukraine War Update - April 28, 2025**

Here are the top news stories around the ongoing conflict between Russia and Ukraine:

**1. Ukraine Launches Counteroffensive in Donetsk Region**
Ukraine's military has launched a counteroffensive in the Donetsk region, aiming to retake control of key cities and towns from Russian-backed separatists. The operation, which began early yesterday, has seen Ukrainian forces make significant gains, with reports of heavy fighting and casualties on both sides. (April 28, 2025)

**2. Russia Accused of War Crimes in Mariupol**
Human rights groups have accused Russia of committing war crimes in the besieged city of Mariupol, where thousands of civilians remain trapped. New evidence suggests that Russian forces have been targeting civilian infrastructure, including hospitals and residential areas, leading to widespread destruction and loss of life. (April 28, 2025)

**3. US Announces Additional Military Aid to Ukraine**
The United States has announced an additional $500 million in military aid to Ukraine, bringing the total package to over $2 billion since the start of the conflict. The new aid package includes advanced weaponry, including anti-tank missiles and drones, to help Ukraine counter Russian aggression. (April 28, 2025)

**4. EU Imposes New Sanctions on Russian Officials**
The European Union has imposed new sanctions on several high-ranking Russian officials, including the country's defense minister, in response to Moscow's continued aggression in Ukraine. The sanctions, which include travel bans and asset freezes, are seen as a significant escalation of EU pressure on Russia. (April 28, 2025)

**5. Ukraine's Zelensky Meets with NATO Leaders in Brussels**
Ukrainian President Volodymyr Zelensky has met with NATO leaders in Brussels to discuss the ongoing conflict and potential membership in the alliance. Zelensky reiterated Ukraine's commitment to joining NATO, while NATO leaders pledged continued support for Ukraine's sovereignty and territorial integrity. (April 28, 2025)

**6. Russia's Economy Suffers Amid Sanctions and War Effort**
Russia's economy is struggling to cope with the impact of Western sanctions and the ongoing war effort in Ukraine. New data shows that Russia's GDP has contracted by over 2% in the first quarter of 2025, with inflation soaring to over 10%. The economic downturn is seen as a major challenge for the Kremlin. (April 28, 2025)
